THE ROSE

The rose, a symbol of beauty, love, and resilience, holds a special place as the state flower of New York. Officially designated in 1955, the rose represents the diversity and strength of New Yorkers, thriving in the state's bustling cities and serene countrysides alike.

What makes the rose particularly fitting is its versatility. With thousands of varieties in almost every color imaginable, it mirrors New York's cultural richness and vibrant character. From the iconic red roses symbolizing passion to delicate white roses evoking purity, the flower's spectrum resonates with the diversity of the Empire State.
