BROWN-EYED SUSAN

Brown-eyed Susan is a hardy perennial native to North America, known for its bright yellow petals and dark brown centers that resemble a "brown eye." Blooming from late summer to fall, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for meadows, gardens, and roadsides.

This drought-tolerant plant attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies and provides lasting color even in the cooler months. With its cheerful appearance and low-maintenance care, it's an excellent addition to pollinator gardens and natural landscapes.
