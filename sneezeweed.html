SNEEZEWEED

Sneezeweed is a vibrant North American perennial known for its late-summer blooms in yellow, orange, or red. Historically, its dried flowers were used as snuff to induce sneezing, believed to expel and ward off evil spirits.

Part of the sunflower family, it thrives in sunny, well-drained areas like meadows and streams, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. While its toxic compounds can harm livestock, sneezeweed is prized in gardens for its low maintenance and autumnal color, often paired with ornamental grasses. It's a hardy plant with both historical and ecological significance.
