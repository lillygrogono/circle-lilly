Swamp rose mallow is a striking perennial native to wetlands in eastern North America, known for its large, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, or red with prominent centers. Blooming in summer, it thrives in moist, sunny areas like marshes and streambanks, attracting bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds.

Despite its tropical appearance, this hardy hibiscus is cold-tolerant and easy to grow, making it a favorite in rain gardens and water-edge plantings. With its vibrant blooms and ecological value, swamp rose mallow brings a dramatic and functional beauty to wetland landscapes.
