DIGITAL CLOCK

On October 23rd, 1956, the first ever digital clock was patented by D.E. Protzmann, marking a major advancement in timekeeping (Pesko).

In 1970, the digital clock evolved into a wearable wrist watch fitted with an LED display, creating the widely recognized clock we know today. Upon many iterations and advancements, the digital clock has become a fundamental aspect of society, deeply enmeshed through our computers, phones, and watches.
