Woodland phlox is a charming perennial native to North American woodlands, known for its fragrant clusters of pale purple to blue flowers that bloom in spring. Preferring partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, it thrives in woodlands, meadows, and garden settings.

This low-maintenance plant attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to wildlife-friendly gardens. With its delicate beauty and natural adaptability, woodland phlox adds a splash of color to shaded areas and is a popular choice for early-season blooms.
