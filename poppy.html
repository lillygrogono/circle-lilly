CELANDINE POPPY

Celandine poppy is a native perennial to North American woodlands, known for its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Thriving in shady, moist areas, it often grows in rich, well-drained soils and is a favorite of early-season pollinators.

With its unique, lobed leaves and striking blooms, celandine poppy adds a pop of color to shaded gardens and forested landscapes. This low-maintenance plant also has a history of medicinal use, although it should be handled with care due to its toxic properties.
