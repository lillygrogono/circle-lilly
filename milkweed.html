COMMON MILKWEED

Common milkweed is a hardy perennial native to North America, known for its fragrant pinkish-purple flower clusters that bloom in summer. Thriving in sunny fields and roadsides, it is a vital host plant for monarch butterflies, providing food for their caterpillars.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, making it ecologically significant. While its milky sap is toxic to some animals, common milkweed is easy to grow and valued for its role in supporting wildlife and restoring native habitats, making it a popular choice for pollinator gardens.
