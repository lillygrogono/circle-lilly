Purple coneflower is a hardy perennial native to North America, known for its striking purple petals and spiky, orange-brown center. Blooming in mid-summer to fall, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is a favorite in gardens, meadows, and prairies.

This drought-tolerant plant attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies and is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in supporting the immune system. With its vibrant flowers and easy care, purple coneflower is a popular addition to pollinator gardens and natural landscapes.
